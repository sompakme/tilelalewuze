The Origins of Siri and Susan Bennett’s Involvement

Siri, the virtual assistant on Apple’s iPhone, has a fascinating origin story. Originally, Siri was an app available for download from Apple’s iOS App Store. However, it was later integrated into the iPhone 4S as a built-in feature. This integration made Siri easily accessible to millions of iPhone users around the world.

But who provided the iconic voice for Siri? That’s where Susan Bennett comes into the picture. In 2005, Susan Bennett, along with other talented voice actors, recorded their voices for Siri under the company ScanSoft, which is now known as Nuance. At that time, the voice actors had no idea that their recordings would be used for the groundbreaking virtual assistant.

To create the voice of Siri, Susan Bennett spent hours meticulously recording nonsensical sentences and sound combinations in the English language. These recordings served as the foundation for Siri’s voice recognition system. Despite the mundane and seemingly random nature of the recordings, they played a crucial role in giving Siri its unique personality and ability to understand and respond to user commands.

“I had absolutely no idea that I was providing the voice for Siri,” mentioned Susan Bennett after discovering her voice had been used for Siri.

It wasn’t until the release of the highly anticipated iPhone 4S in 2011 that Susan Bennett realized her voice had been chosen as the voice of Siri. Users around the world were introduced to the friendly and helpful voice that would become a staple of the Apple ecosystem.

Susan Bennett’s unwitting involvement in shaping one of the most recognizable voices in technology is a testament to her talent as a voice actor. Her dedication to delivering a flawless performance during the recording process laid the groundwork for Siri’s success and set a new standard for virtual assistants.

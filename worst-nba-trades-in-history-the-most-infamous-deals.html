Worst NBA Trades in History: The Most Infamous Deals

The NBA has witnessed its fair share of disastrous player trades over the years. From trades that crippled teams to deals that saw future superstars slip through their fingers, these trades have become infamous within the NBA community. In this article, we will delve into the background and aftermath of some of the worst NBA trades in history. Get ready to explore the biggest trade flops and most regrettable deals that have left a lasting impact on the teams involved.

  • The NBA has seen its fair share of regrettable trades, with some deals having long-lasting negative effects on the teams involved.
  • Trade missteps range from missing out on future superstars to giving away key players in lopsided deals.
  • These trades serve as cautionary tales for teams, reminding them to carefully consider the long-term implications of their trade decisions to avoid becoming a part of the history of the worst NBA trades.

Jazz trade away Magic Johnson without realizing it

In 1978, the Utah Jazz made a trade with the Los Angeles Lakers that would come back to haunt them. The Jazz traded away Sam Worthen, Freemen Williams, and Kenny Carr in exchange for Gail Goodrich, Jack Givens, Essie Givens, and a first-round pick.

Little did the Jazz know that this trade would result in missing out on one of the NBA’s greatest players. Due to the Jazz finishing last in the season, the Lakers were able to use that first-round pick to select Magic Johnson, who would go on to have an illustrious career, winning multiple championships with the Lakers.

This trade is often regarded as one of the worst in NBA history, as the Jazz missed the opportunity to acquire a player of Johnson’s caliber and the immense success he brought to the Lakers.

The Impact:

  • Utah Jazz: The Jazz traded away valuable assets and missed out on the chance to have Magic Johnson as a cornerstone of their franchise. This trade significantly hindered the Jazz’s ability to compete at a high level and left a lasting impact on their future success.
  • Magic Johnson: Johnson’s career with the Lakers propelled him to superstar status, becoming one of the most iconic players in NBA history. Winning multiple championships, Johnson’s impact on the Lakers is legendary, and the Jazz trade only added to his legacy.
  • Los Angeles Lakers: The Lakers benefitted greatly from the Jazz trade, securing a future Hall of Famer and one of the greatest point guards of all time. Johnson played a crucial role in the Lakers’ success and helped solidify their dynasty throughout the 1980s.

Despite the passage of time, the Jazz trade for Goodrich, Givens, and a first-round pick remains one of the most regrettable moves in NBA history. The missed opportunity to acquire a player of Magic Johnson’s caliber highlights the importance of carefully evaluating trades and understanding the long-term implications for the future success of a franchise.

Nets trade for aging Celtics core for haul of draft picks

In an ambitious move aimed at propelling the Brooklyn Nets to championship contention, the team made a trade with the Boston Celtics that involved acquiring an aging core of veterans. The Nets traded for Kevin Garnett, Paul Pierce, Jason Terry, and Kris Joseph from the Celtics, while the Celtics received role players and a significant number of future first-round draft picks in return. However, this ill-advised trade proved to be heavily lopsided in favor of the Celtics.

The Brooklyn Nets had hoped that the experience and championship pedigree of Garnett and Pierce would provide the leadership and skill needed to make a deep playoff run. Unfortunately, the aging core failed to produce the desired results on the court. The team struggled in the playoffs and was unable to achieve the success they had envisioned. Meanwhile, the Boston Celtics capitalized on the draft picks they received from the Nets to rebuild their roster and lay the foundation for future success.

The Celtics used those future first-round draft picks to select promising young talents Jaylen Brown and Jayson Tatum. Brown and Tatum have since emerged as integral pieces of the Celtics’ roster, forming a dynamic duo that has propelled the team back to competitiveness and playoff contention. Their contributions have put the Celtics in a much stronger position, both in the present and for the future.

This trade serves as a cautionary tale for teams in the NBA, reminding them of the potential risks and consequences of making ill-advised trades. The Nets’ decision to trade for an aging core at the expense of valuable draft picks ultimately set them back and hindered their progress. Meanwhile, the Celtics’ shrewd maneuvering and astute use of those draft picks allowed them to retool their roster and position themselves for sustained success.

Despite the initial excitement surrounding the trade, it became clear that the Nets had made a significant miscalculation. The Celtics emerged as clear winners, while the Nets were left grappling with the consequences of their ill-advised decision. This trade serves as a stark reminder that teams must carefully evaluate the potential long-term implications of their trades, especially when it involves sacrificing valuable assets like draft picks.

Bucks trade Dirk Nowitzki for Robert Traylor

In 1998, the Milwaukee Bucks made a trade that would go down as one of the worst in NBA history. They sent a young Dirk Nowitzki, who would later become one of the greatest power forwards of all time, to the Dallas Mavericks in exchange for Robert Traylor.

At the time of the trade, Nowitzki was a promising rookie with tremendous potential. Traylor, on the other hand, had already shown signs of inconsistency and struggled to make an impact on the court. The talent disparity between the two players was glaring, making this trade a head-scratcher for many basketball enthusiasts.

Nowitzki went on to have a legendary career with the Mavericks, becoming a 14-time All-Star, winning an MVP award, and leading his team to an NBA championship in 2011. Traylor, on the other hand, failed to live up to expectations and played just six seasons in the NBA before retiring.

This trade serves as a prime example of how a team can miss out on a future superstar by undervaluing their potential. It is often cited as one of the most lopsided trades in NBA history, and a cautionary tale for teams to thoroughly evaluate the long-term consequences before making any major deals.

Key Takeaways:

  • The Milwaukee Bucks traded Dirk Nowitzki to the Dallas Mavericks for Robert Traylor in 1998.
  • Nowitzki went on to become one of the greatest power forwards in NBA history, while Traylor had a short and unremarkable career.
  • This trade is considered one of the worst in NBA history due to the immense talent disparity between the players involved.

Supersonics trade Scottie Pippen for Olden Polynice

In a move that would have significant implications for both franchises, the Seattle Supersonics drafted Scottie Pippen with the fifth overall pick in the 1987 draft. However, they quickly traded him to the Chicago Bulls for help at the center position in the form of Olden Polynice.

Pippen went on to become a key player in the Bulls’ dynasty, winning six championships alongside Michael Jordan. This trade is often regarded as one of the worst in NBA history due to the missed opportunity to build a formidable team around Pippen.

Despite Pippen’s tremendous success with the Bulls, the Supersonics missed out on his potential to become an integral part of their franchise. This trade serves as a cautionary tale, reminding teams of the importance of thoroughly evaluating the long-term implications of their trades.

Missed Opportunity for the Supersonics

The Supersonics had a chance to build a strong team around Pippen, who had shown immense potential as a versatile and talented player. His ability to contribute on both ends of the court made him a valuable asset and a future superstar in the making.

However, the Supersonics opted to address their need for a center by acquiring Polynice, a player who did not have the same impact or career trajectory as Pippen. This decision ultimately hindered the Supersonics’ ability to compete at the highest level and establish a championship-contending team.

Pippen’s Impact with the Bulls

On the other hand, Pippen’s tenure with the Bulls propelled the team to unprecedented success. Alongside Michael Jordan, Pippen formed one of the most iconic duos in NBA history. Their combination of versatility, skill, and tenacity led the Bulls to six NBA championships in the 1990s.

Pippen’s contributions went beyond scoring and defense. His playmaking ability, basketball IQ, and leadership qualities played a crucial role in the Bulls’ dominance during their championship runs. He cemented his status as one of the greatest small forwards in NBA history and a vital piece of the Bulls’ dynasty.

Lessons Learned

The Supersonics’ trade of Scottie Pippen for Olden Polynice serves as a cautionary tale in the NBA. It highlights the importance of carefully evaluating the long-term implications of trades and considering the potential impact of acquiring or giving up a future superstar.

Teams must prioritize building a strong foundation and surrounding their talented players with the right pieces to maximize their potential. Failing to do so can lead to missed opportunities, hindered growth, and, in some cases, becoming a part of the history of the worst NBA trades.

Sixers trade Charles Barkley for Jeff Hornacek and bench players

In a move that left fans scratching their heads, the Philadelphia 76ers made a trade that would go down as one of the worst in NBA history. They traded away their star player, Charles Barkley, to the Phoenix Suns in exchange for Jeff Hornacek, Tim Perry, and Andrew Kang.

Charles Barkley was the heart and soul of the Sixers, a dominant force on the court who had become a fan favorite in Philadelphia. He was a perennial All-Star and had established himself as one of the best players in the league.

On the other hand, Jeff Hornacek, Tim Perry, and Andrew Kang were role players who failed to make a significant impact on the Sixers’ roster. Hornacek had a decent career, but he was nowhere near the player Barkley was.

The trade turned out to be a huge mistake for the Sixers, as Barkley went on to achieve great success with the Phoenix Suns. He won the MVP award in the 1992-93 season and led the Suns to the NBA Finals in 1993. Meanwhile, the Sixers struggled to fill the void left by Barkley’s departure.

This trade serves as a cautionary tale for NBA teams, highlighting the importance of carefully evaluating the value of players and potential returns in trades. Trading away a player of Barkley’s caliber for a package that failed to deliver is a decision that the Sixers undoubtedly regret.

Rockets trade youth for aging Charles Barkley

After achieving back-to-back NBA championships in 1994 and 1995, the Houston Rockets made a trade that is widely regarded as a major misstep. In an attempt to bolster their roster and continue their championship run, the Rockets traded away a group of young depth players to the Utah Jazz in exchange for the aging star Charles Barkley.

The trade, executed in 1996, saw the Rockets part ways with promising talents Sam Cassell, Robert Horry, Chucky Brown, and Mark Bryant. These players had contributed significantly to the team’s success and were seen as integral pieces of their young core.

While Charles Barkley had an illustrious career and was undoubtedly a talented player, the Rockets failed to capture another championship with him in their lineup. Barkley’s time with the Rockets was marked by injuries and declining performance, leaving the team with limited young talent and hindering their ability to sustain their previous success.

This trade has since become one of the worst in NBA history, as it represents a missed opportunity for the Rockets to continue their dominant run with their young core intact. It serves as a reminder of the risks associated with trading valuable young players for aging stars, as the long-term implications can be detrimental to a team’s future success.

Bucks trade Kareem Abdul-Jabbar for unknowns

In a move that would prove to be a major blunder, the Milwaukee Bucks traded away Kareem Abdul-Jabbar in 1975. In return, they received Elmore Smith, Brian Winters, Junior Bridgeman, and Dave Meyers. While these players had solid careers, they were nowhere near the level of Abdul-Jabbar, who went on to achieve legendary status with the Los Angeles Lakers.

This trade is often mentioned as one of the worst in NBA history due to the immense talent disparity and the impact it had on both franchises. Abdul-Jabbar became one of the greatest players of all time, winning multiple championships and earning numerous accolades, while the players acquired by the Bucks failed to reach the same level of success.

The Bucks’ decision to trade away Abdul-Jabbar had long-lasting repercussions, highlighting the importance of carefully evaluating trades and considering the long-term implications for a team’s success.

St. Louis Hawks trade away Bill Russell

In a move that would haunt the St. Louis Hawks for years to come, they traded away Bill Russell to the Boston Celtics. The return for Russell included Ed Macauley and Cliff Hagan, both of whom had solid careers but couldn’t match Russell’s impact. Russell went on to become one of the greatest players in NBA history, leading the Celtics to numerous championships. This trade is often considered one of the worst in NBA history due to the missed opportunity to build a dynasty around Russell.

Bill Russell was a game-changer in the NBA, known for his exceptional defensive skills and leadership abilities. After being drafted by the St. Louis Hawks in 1956, Russell was quickly traded to the Boston Celtics in a move that would shape the course of NBA history. The Celtics capitalized on Russell’s talents and went on to dominate the league, winning a remarkable 11 championships during his 13-year tenure with the team.

Meanwhile, the St. Louis Hawks, despite having a talented roster, failed to find the same success. Ed Macauley and Cliff Hagan, the players acquired in the trade, had respectable careers, but they couldn’t replicate Russell’s impact on the court. The Hawks missed out on the opportunity to build a dynasty around a player of Russell’s caliber, making this trade one of the most regrettable in NBA history.

Bill Russell’s legacy in the NBA is unmatched. Not only did he revolutionize the game with his defensive prowess and shot-blocking ability, but he also paved the way for future generations of African American players. Russell’s impact on and off the court cannot be understated, making the St. Louis Hawks’ decision to trade him away even more baffling in retrospect.

Hornets trade Kobe Bryant for Vlade Divac

In 1996, the Charlotte Hornets drafted a young and promising player named Kobe Bryant. However, they made the unfortunate decision to trade him to the Los Angeles Lakers in exchange for Vlade Divac. While Divac had a successful career and made valuable contributions to the Hornets, this trade is widely regarded as one of the worst in NBA history.

Kobe Bryant went on to become one of the greatest players to ever grace the basketball court. He won five championships with the Lakers, earned numerous accolades, and solidified his status as a basketball legend. His impact on the game and the Lakers’ success cannot be overstated.

Meanwhile, the Hornets missed out on the opportunity to have a future Hall of Famer and one of the most dominant forces in the history of the sport. This trade serves as a reminder of the potential consequences of trading away young talent and failing to recognize their true potential.

The Hornets’ decision to trade Kobe Bryant for Vlade Divac is often mentioned among the worst NBA trades of all time. It stands as a cautionary tale for teams, highlighting the importance of evaluating player potential and investing in the future.
